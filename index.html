Why do we care?

For decades, tobacco smoking has stood as one of the most significant global health challenges.

In the 20th century alone, it claimed nearly 100 million lives—primarily in countries now considered affluent.

Today, however, the health impacts of smoking are increasingly shifting toward low- and middle-income nations.

"With some projections estimating that tobacco could cause up to one billion deaths in the 21st century."

Conclusion

Smoking remains one of the deadliest habits, claiming millions of lives globally. However, strict tobacco policies—higher taxes, advertising bans, and smoking restrictions—are proving effective. Data shows a steady decline in smoking-related deaths as governments take stronger action against tobacco. While progress is being made, continued awareness, regulation, and personal choices are key to further reducing smoking’s deadly impact.
